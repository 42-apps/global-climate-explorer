About the Global Climate Explorer

This map paints the climate of every point on Earth — blue where it's cold, green where it's most comfortable, yellow for warm, red where it's too hot — for any month of the year. Drag the timeline to find the best places to be in January, July, or any month; or pick Year-round for the best all-year climates.

The default ⭐ Comfort view is evidence-based: it scores each place on how comfortable and healthy its climate is for humans. Switch to 🌡️ Temperature, 💧 Humidity or 🌧️ Rainfall to map just that. Or open ⚙️ Your ideal climate to override the autopilot — chase the cold to ski, the rain for green hills, or heat & dry — and the map & rankings rebuild around you.

How the comfort score works

Climate figures are 1970–2000 monthly normals from WorldClim 2.1 (~18 km resolution), with relative humidity derived from vapour pressure. Cities are every settlement above 100,000 people (GeoNames). This shows the typical climate, not a forecast, and predates the most recent warming. Comfort is one defensible model among many — your mileage (and your sweater) may vary.
